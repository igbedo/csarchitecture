Why organizations sponsor Fellows

Cloud platforms are becoming more complex, and AI expands both capability and attack surface. Many teams have tool proficiency but lack security-first architectural depth — resulting in fragile systems, noisy alerting, manual operations, and inconsistent design decisions.

This sponsorship pathway develops engineers into security-first cloud architects who can design under constraints, automate delivery end-to-end, and defend technical decisions in review.

Outcomes employers value

  • Risk reduction through security-first architecture (IAM, segmentation, telemetry)
  • Lower operational toil via automation-only delivery (IaC + CI/CD)
  • Better decision quality through tradeoff-based architectural reasoning
  • Improved signal quality through detection + response readiness
  • Responsible AI adoption with validation and guardrail design

What sponsorship includes

Employer sponsorship seats are designed to be procurement-friendly, measurable, and aligned to professional development outcomes.

Seat + evaluation

Fellow participates in the 16-week program with rubric-based evaluation across architecture, automation, security, detection, and AI-augmented operations.

Capstone artifacts

The Fellow produces architecture documentation and implementation artifacts suitable for internal review — diagram, design memo, and automation repo structure.

Defense-based credentialing

The ACSA-F designation is awarded only after successful capstone defense. Not all admitted Fellows earn the designation.

Optional employer reporting

For employer-sponsored seats, the Institute can provide a concise performance summary aligned to evaluation domains and capstone completion status.
